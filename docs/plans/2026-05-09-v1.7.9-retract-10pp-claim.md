## Why pre-emptive retraction (deliberate departure from v1.7.7 pre-reg)

The v1.7.7 prereg and `CHANGELOG.md:41-43` read: "The hard-stop retraction clause fires on NOT_SUPPORTED, not SANITY_FAIL — v1.7.7 was SANITY_FAIL. If v1.8 also fails, the magnitude claim should be treated as falsified."

That wording made retraction conditional on v1.8 outcome. v1.7.9 retracts EARLIER. Reasoning (must appear in the v1.7.9 CHANGELOG entry verbatim):

1. **Cumulative evidence is informative without v1.8.** Three pre-registered workload variants tested across distinct knobs (saturation, budget, restricted-window). Every C2 hippo-base late mean returned 0% across every seed.

2. **The v1.7.7 prereg explicitly distinguished SANITY_FAIL (no retraction) from NOT_SUPPORTED (retraction).** v1.7.9 deviates from that distinction on cumulative-evidence grounds. The deviation is declared, not silent.

3. **The original prereg's distinction was wrong.** Three SANITY_FAILs across distinct workload knobs is meaningful negative evidence regardless of formal verdict label. v1.7.9 corrects that prereg design choice. (This is not "more conservative than the pre-reg required" — it is a different choice that prioritises public-surface honesty over completing the full pre-registered escalation chain.)

4. **Decoupling protects against v1.8 timing.** v1.8 is multi-hour eval work with new category authoring + harness tweaks. If v1.8 slips, the false magnitude claim should not stay live in the README and ROADMAP-RESEARCH.

5. **CLAUDE.md "Honest Reporting" requires it.** "If results are null, flat, or worse than the baseline, say so plainly. Do not soften, frame around it, or lead with the one positive metric." Three nulls across three pre-registered variants IS a null. The mechanism stays shipped; the magnitude is a separate claim. v1.8 still runs as planned; the retraction is independent of v1.8 outcome.

This departure is documented explicitly in the v1.7.9 CHANGELOG entry. It is NOT silent or selective retraction.
